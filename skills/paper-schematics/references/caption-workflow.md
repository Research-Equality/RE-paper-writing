# Caption Workflow

Good captions let the figure stand alone without turning into a second methods section.

## Caption Structure

1. One-sentence headline: what the figure shows
2. Reading guide: how to follow panels or flow
3. Definitions: symbols, abbreviations, color semantics
4. Special notes: counts, exclusions, or conceptual-versus-empirical distinction

## Caption Checks

- Does the caption explain the figure's main claim in one sentence?
- Can a reviewer understand every panel label without hunting through the text?
- Does the caption avoid repeating obvious visual content line by line?
- If the figure contains counts or process steps, do they match the manuscript exactly?

## Failure Modes

- Caption only names the figure without explaining it
- Caption depends on unstated abbreviations
- Caption overclaims what the figure proves
- Caption silently mixes conceptual illustrations with measured results
