# Score Interpretation

## Default Weighting

- `problem_formulation`: 15%
- `literature_review`: 15%
- `methodology`: 20%
- `data_collection`: 10%
- `analysis`: 15%
- `results`: 10%
- `writing`: 10%
- `citations`: 5%

## Overall Thresholds

- `4.5-5.0`: Exceptional, close to top-tier readiness
- `4.0-4.4`: Strong, minor revisions
- `3.5-3.9`: Good foundation, major revisions still needed
- `3.0-3.4`: Acceptable but significant revision required
- `2.0-2.9`: Weak, major rework
- `<2.0`: Not ready

## Tracking Rule

- Compare versions only when the scoring scope is the same.
- Keep both the raw JSON and rendered report.
- Look for movement in weak dimensions first; average score alone can hide regressions.

## Common Misuse

- Giving high `writing` and assuming the paper is submission-ready
- Letting one reviewer assign numbers without explaining evidence
- Comparing a section-only score against a full-manuscript score
