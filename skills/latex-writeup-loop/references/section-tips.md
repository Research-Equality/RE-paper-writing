# Section Tips

These tips are distilled from the AI Scientist writeup loop and are intended for template-first manuscript drafting.

## Abstract

- State what the paper does and why it matters.
- Explain why the problem is hard.
- Summarize the core contribution and the strongest evidence.
- Keep it as one continuous paragraph.

## Introduction

- Expand the abstract into the full paper story.
- State motivation, challenge, approach, and evidence.
- Include an explicit contribution list when appropriate.

## Related Work

- Compare and contrast prior work with the current method.
- If a prior method is experimentally relevant, explain whether it is compared directly.
- Do not reduce the section to disconnected paper summaries.

## Background

- Introduce the concepts and notation needed to understand the method.
- Separate true prerequisites from the paper's own contributions.

## Method

- Explain what the method does and why the design choices exist.
- Build on the notation introduced earlier instead of redefining everything.

## Experimental Setup

- Describe datasets, metrics, baselines, and key implementation details.
- Do not invent unavailable hardware or training details.

## Results

- Only report saved, reproducible experimental outcomes.
- Discuss baselines, fairness, limitations, and figure-backed findings.
- Include all relevant existing figures rather than only the most flattering ones.

## Conclusion

- Recap the core finding.
- State realistic limitations and next steps.
