# High-Risk Zones

Unsupported claims do the most damage in these locations:

- title
- abstract
- first two introduction paragraphs
- contribution bullet list
- conclusion summary sentences

## Why These Zones Matter

- Reviewers form early trust judgments from them.
- Claims in these zones often get quoted back in reviews.
- If the support is weak here, later evidence may not recover credibility.

## Typical Risk Signals

- "significantly outperforms" without a visible comparison anchor
- "generalizes well" without hard-setting or out-of-domain evidence
- "robust" without failure-case or perturbation testing
- "simple yet effective" when complexity and tradeoffs are not shown
- "first" or "novel" when novelty is asserted but not framed carefully
