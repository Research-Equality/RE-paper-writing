# Reviewer Priorities

Anticipate objections before the paper enters review.

## High-Impact Journals

- First question: "Why does this matter broadly?"
- Common rejection reasons:
  - important but too incremental
  - claims not fully supported
  - inaccessible writing for non-specialists

## ML and AI Conferences

- First question: "Is the method sound and convincingly better than strong baselines?"
- Common rejection reasons:
  - weak or outdated baselines
  - missing ablations
  - narrow evaluation
  - poor reproducibility detail

## Medical Journals

- First question: "Will this change understanding or practice, and is the analysis defensible?"
- Common rejection reasons:
  - underpowered study
  - unclear comparator
  - incomplete reporting
  - claims that exceed the evidence

## Cell-Style and Mechanism-Heavy Biology Venues

- First question: "What is the mechanism, and how complete is the evidence?"
- Common rejection reasons:
  - no rescue or orthogonal validation
  - representative images without quantification
  - incomplete control set

## Practical Use

- Rewrite abstract and introduction so they answer the venue's first question immediately.
- Keep the most venue-relevant evidence in the main paper.
- Use rebuttal language that matches the venue's review culture, not a generic response template.
