# Venue Families

Use venue family as the first routing decision when the exact venue is known or when the user is comparing options.

## High-Impact Journals

- Examples: `Nature`, `Science`, `Cell`
- Optimize for: broad significance, novelty, clear story for non-specialists
- Writing implication: lead with why the result matters outside the immediate niche
- Structural implication: main paper must carry significance early, not hide it in later sections

## ML and AI Conferences

- Examples: `NeurIPS`, `ICML`, `ICLR`, `CVPR`, `ACL`
- Optimize for: technical soundness, fair baselines, ablations, reproducibility, novelty
- Writing implication: contribution must survive comparison to recent strong baselines
- Structural implication: main paper needs methods clarity and convincing experiments under page limits

## Medical and Clinical Journals

- Examples: `NEJM`, `Lancet`, `JAMA`, specialty clinical journals
- Optimize for: clinical relevance, methodology, patient outcomes, statistical validity, generalizability
- Writing implication: claims must stay aligned with evidence and outcome definition
- Structural implication: reporting standards and structured abstracts often matter

## HCI and Design Research

- Examples: `CHI`, `CSCW`
- Optimize for: contribution type, user-centered rationale, appropriate evaluation, implications
- Writing implication: match claims to the evaluation actually performed
- Structural implication: related work and discussion often need stronger framing around design implications

## Specialist Journals

- Examples: domain-specific bioinformatics, systems, or applied journals
- Optimize for: field relevance, methodological rigor, completeness within the niche
- Writing implication: domain terminology and benchmark expectations matter more than broad appeal
- Structural implication: more space may exist for detailed methods and domain context

## Routing Rule

- If the user has no target venue, pick the closest venue family first.
- If two venues imply different paper stories, write a short comparison before choosing one.
