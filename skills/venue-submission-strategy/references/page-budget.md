# Page Budget

Page limits create writing tradeoffs before they create formatting problems.

## Main-Paper Priorities

- Protect space for the contribution statement, core evidence, and the one figure or table reviewers will remember.
- Move implementation details, exhaustive ablations, or peripheral analyses to appendix only after the main claim is secure.
- Keep all evidence required to believe the headline claim inside the main paper.

## Budgeting Questions

- What must a reviewer see to judge the contribution as credible?
- Which experiment is optional polish versus decision-critical evidence?
- Can Figure 1 or Table 1 replace a paragraph of explanation?
- Does the appendix hold support, or is it hiding something essential?

## Common Failure Modes

- Spending too much space on background and too little on evidence
- Overloading the main paper with minor ablations while omitting essential comparisons
- Writing for a long-form journal and then compressing mechanically for a short conference paper
- Discovering too late that anonymization or template rules force major structural edits

## Useful Heuristic

- Abstract and first figure should survive even if the reviewer only skims the first two pages.
- Methods detail can move later; credibility signals cannot.
