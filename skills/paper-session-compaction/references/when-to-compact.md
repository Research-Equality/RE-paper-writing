# When to Compact

## Good Times to Compact

- the session has accumulated many resolved planning turns
- context overflow is likely or already happened
- the work is shifting from one phase to another, such as drafting to revision
- you want a checkpoint before handing work to a later session or collaborator

## Bad Times to Compact

- the main disagreement is still unresolved
- the active task depends on exact wording from the immediately previous turns
- the artifact paths or pending edits are still changing rapidly

## Recovery Rule

- If a later session cannot act confidently from the continuation brief, the summary was too lossy.
- In that case, reopen the latest draft plus the missing source artifact and patch the brief before continuing.
