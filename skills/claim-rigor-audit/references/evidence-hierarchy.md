# Evidence Hierarchy

Judge the strength of a manuscript claim from the evidence type carrying it.

## Typical Order

1. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
2. Randomized controlled trials
3. Cohort studies
4. Case-control studies
5. Cross-sectional studies
6. Case reports and case series
7. Expert opinion

## Audit Questions

- Is the study design capable of supporting the claimed inference?
- Are the authors using observational evidence to make causal claims?
- Are the included studies or data sources good enough to justify strong language?
- Does a high evidence tier still have serious flaws that should downgrade confidence?

## Important Nuance

- Higher-tier evidence can still be weak if randomization, blinding, attrition handling, or search strategy is poor.
- Lower-tier evidence can still matter, but the manuscript language must stay calibrated to the uncertainty.
