# Paragraph Relations

When checking flow, identify the relation between adjacent sentences or paragraphs.

## Preferred Relation Types

- `cause`: explains why the previous statement happens
- `contrast`: introduces a limitation, exception, or alternative
- `consequence`: states what follows from the previous claim
- `refinement`: narrows, sharpens, or disambiguates the previous statement
- `example`: concretizes an abstract statement

## Flow Questions

- Does the first sentence state the paragraph's message?
- Can each sentence be linked to the previous one by one of the relation types above?
- Does the paragraph end with a sentence that naturally opens the next paragraph?
- Are there abrupt noun or concept jumps that need a bridging sentence?

## Rewrite Triggers

Rewrite when:

- the paragraph has more than one message
- the first sentence does not signal the paragraph role
- a sentence changes topic without a clear relation
- the paragraph could be moved elsewhere with no loss of coherence
