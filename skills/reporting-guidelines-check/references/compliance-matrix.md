# Compliance Matrix

Build a working table like this:

| Item | Status | Evidence | Location | Required Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary outcome definition | partial | outcome named, not timed | Methods | specify timing and measurement rule |
| Participant flow | missing | none | -- | add CONSORT diagram + denominators |
| Data availability | complete | repository DOI present | end matter | none |

## Section Audit Order

### Title and Abstract

- Does the title reveal the study type when the guideline expects it?
- Does the abstract include the core design, population, primary endpoint, and headline result?

### Introduction

- Is the rationale strong enough to justify the design?
- Are objectives or hypotheses explicit?

### Methods

- Are participants, datasets, or samples defined precisely?
- Are inclusion and exclusion criteria stated?
- Are interventions, preprocessing, or measurement procedures reproducible?
- Are statistics or evaluation metrics justified?
- Are randomization, blinding, confounding control, or missing-data handling covered when relevant?

### Results

- Are denominators, counts, and flow through the study visible?
- Are all primary outcomes reported, not just favorable ones?
- Are uncertainty measures included where expected?
- Are adverse events, failure cases, or negative results covered where required?

### Discussion and End Matter

- Are limitations specific rather than generic?
- Are registration, ethics, funding, conflicts, and data or code availability stated?
- Are supplementary materials referenced when they carry checklist evidence?

## Common Missing Items

- Registration number or protocol link
- Flow diagram for trials and reviews
- Sample-size determination or power rationale
- Clear handling of exclusions and missing data
- Confidence intervals or effect sizes
- Data and code availability statements
- Bias or limitation discussion that matches the actual design
